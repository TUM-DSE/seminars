## Course Details

This course will introduce advanced topics in computer systems, including operating systems, distributed systems, file systems, networked systems, virtualization, and confidential computing. System software forms the bedrock of our computing infrastructure. The participants will learn about computer system designs and emerging trends in real-world systems, including data-centers comprising 100s of thousands of machines. Such systems are operated by Amazon AWS, Google Cloud, Microsoft Azure, and Facebook, many of whom heavily rely on virtual machines (VMs) to manage systems and are also involved in the development of hypervisors and OSes. The topics covered in this course include VMs for serverless, networking for VMs, specialized hardware for VMs and security properties of VMs (as opposed to containerization).

The participants will be able to build on their knowledge in the core courses and apply the fundamental course concepts in the recent advances in the broad field of computer systems, which mainly covers areas such as operating systems, virtualization technology, or distributed systems, but also interfaces to related areas like security, networking, computer architecture, and software reliability.


## OBjective (expected results of study and acquired competences):

The participants will read, discuss, and analyze recent papers from top conferences in this field, particularly ASPLOS, OSDI, USENIX ATC, and EuroSys. Participants are expected to read papers in depth prior to class and take turns preparing and delivering high-quality presentations of the papers. Furthermore, the participants will actively participate in the discussion to critically analyze the system and advance the state of the art. More concretely, the participants will learn:
- Advanced scientific topics in computer systems.
- Scientific report preparation
- Presentation and discussion of scientific results
- Peer-review of the scientific reports

## Teaching and learning method (transfer of skills) workload for students:

We will assign papers for discussion and research study. The participants will work in a team of two
participants and hold *two presentations* in person. More specifically, the course will be organized in
three stages:

1. **Papers presentation phase:** In this phase, the participants will present the research papers assigned
to them. They should clearly understand and explain the context of the work, contributions, and
potential future work.
2. **Research phase:** In the second phase, the student will work on the identified research problem
statement. The research phase should clearly motivate the research problem, and also should clearly
explain the research methodology to solve the problem. The participants will present their research ideas.
3. **Reports and peer-review**:  As the last phase, the participants are expected to submit a report
summarizing the presented paper and the research proposal. Further, we will be using the state-of-the-art
reviewing system (hotcrp) for peer review of the reports. In the peer-review process, the participants
are expected to give feedback covering the positive and critical aspects.


We recommend that the participants devote roughly equal amounts of time to each of these phases. Lastly,
the fellow participants are expected to read the paper and engage the presenters in an active discussion.
